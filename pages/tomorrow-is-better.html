Tomorrow is better

By Israa Al-Ghaithi
June 30, 2019

Wow! I didn't know that reviewing books was this hard. However, I'm going to try it so please bear with me. Among the books I have read so far, I wanted to give you a little push of motivation through this book the same you it did me.

Title: Tomorrow is better
Author: Abdullah AlMaghlooth
Genre: Self development | Self-development and relationships
Published On: 2017
Publisher: Madarek Publishing House
Source: Library
Pages: 182

Synopsis

Tomorrow is more beautiful - an invitation to bring joy and happiness to yourself, not to stand in the face of the disappointments of time that you are exposed to, and to stay away from loneliness. An invitation to share your sorrows with your friends. There are many simple things that bring a smile to your lips, such as playing with a young child, remembering some old funny memories, and other situations that you have experienced.

Introduction

I did not expect a gift like this from my friend would make my tomorrow a better day.

Taste of Soul thoughts

It is strange in this life that there is no secret in it, everything is clear just like the problems and their solutions, but we do not deny that falling into the situation and trying to get out of the danger zone to safety is a difficult matter to do because of our human nature and its strange emotions. This book gives you some solutions or ideas that you may do to reduce the impact of these difficulties, challenges, and psychological problems that you may experience in a direct and humor way. Examples of this are when the author discussed the topic of personal/job interviews, how to prepare for them, the possible questions that you may face during interviews, and how to relieve your stress. Or when he provided us with simple idea to make us laugh and smile through the day, for example, make fun of yourself or put a funny wallpaper on your mobile screen. What truly affected me the most and made me think about it was when he said:

Do not compare yourself to anyone. Our presence on social networks has made us compare ourselves to others. Do not be fooled by the screen, everyone suffers.
