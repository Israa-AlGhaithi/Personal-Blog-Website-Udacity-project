Concrete Art

By Israa Al-Ghaithi
Jan 7, 2024

Yesterday I attended a concrete art workshop that happened in my hometown which was a surprised gift from my sister, and it was one of the most entertaining workshop I have ever attended. In case if you don't know what concrete art is? Is basically the use of gypsum to create crafts, home decor such as vases, cups and plates etc. In this workshop we used a special handmade white powder that was made by the team of this workshop which distinguishes as the best and fastest in drying and doesn't need to scrub.

Taste of Soul Fun fact: They pre-packed a box for us with all the equipment we would need for this workshop. This box contains one kilogram of gypsum bag, one mica powder color, 2 cups, and 2 wooden sticks.

How to use

step 1:

Coach Narjis taught us how to make crafts using this magic powder. She provided us with other equipment other than that box, such as a scale, coloring brushes, shape molds, and other colors. The first rule is that for every 100 grams of gypsum requires 30 ml of water. In our case, we used 200g of gypsum which means we used 60 ml of water and of course we used the scale to weigh the gypsum. Next step is to slowly add water into the gypsum (not the other way around) while stirring continuously with the wooden stick until the mixture become a little bit thick and smooth to use.


Taste of Soul P.S now please remember if you want to use a colored gypsum using Mica powder color you have to add and mix mica powder to the gypsum powder before adding the water.

step 2:

After that, is pouring the mixture into any shape of mold we want to use. These molds are made of silicone which are more flexible, easy to work with and easy to remove once a part is fully formed. For me, I chose these wonderful shapes, they are beautiful, aren't they? And of course, we have to wait for approximately 30 minutes for them to dry.

step 3:

Now everything is ready to decorate. Before coloring or drawing I applied the Varnish (A glossy liquid to protect paintings and artifact crafts) across my pieces. You can use acrylic colors or any other decoration methods to complete your pieces of art if needed. Yay! I'm so satisfied with the final results.
