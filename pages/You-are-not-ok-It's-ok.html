You are not ok? It's ok!

By Israa Al-Ghaithi
May 7, 2023

To say I'm obsessed with reading is quite underestimated. I really love reading but unfortunately sometimes it is hard to find the time for it. But even time has not stop me from reading approximately 15 books in 2023, I'm patting myself right now. One of the books that influenced me is the book It's OK Not to Be OK and I have read the translated version so I'm quite hesitant in reviewing it.

Title: You are not ok? It's ok!(Original: It's OK Not to Be OK)
Author: Claire Chamberlain
Genre: Self-Care, Health & Well Being, Self Help, Self-Improvement, Self-Esteem
Published On: 2022
Publisher: Dar Al Saqi
Source: Library
Pages: 159

Synopsis

What's normal anyway?

Into every life some rain must fall. Nobody is fine all the time, and if you're feeling down or struggling with serious problems, you're not alone. This clear and comforting guide is here to help you understand the mental health issues that can affect us all, and to help you look after your mind, body and soul. Touching on a range of topics, including anxiety, depression, loneliness, stress and self-esteem, this is a book for anyone and everyone who needs good advice, fresh ideas and kind words.

Introduction

The moment I walked through the bookshelves, the simplicity, size and the title of this book is what attracted me to it. I mean, “You are not ok? It is ok” Just from this phrase alone it makes you feel that everything is ok, don't you think so? Frankly, I was amazed by the simplicity and the beauty of the way this book delivered its words and messages.

Taste of Soul thoughts

I love how this book is frank and clear in conveying its message to the reader's soul and providing us with simple words that have a powerful impact in changing our psychological and mental state at that moment. The calmness and reassurance that this book sends warmly runs through the veins of your body. This book gently pats you on the shoulder and teaches you how to deal with situations, your body, and your emotions in an easy and direct way without imposing force. It's delightful to read such a bright book where you feel that everyone is exactly feeling the same way you feel. Another feature that I loved about this book is that Claire not only wrote her own words, but also included wonderful words from other people. These are some quotes that I liked, of course there are more but I don't want to spoiler the beauty of them for you:

Embrace the glorious mess that you are - Elizabeth Gilbert

Take care of your body- It's the only place you have to live - Jim Rohn.

Sleep is the best meditation - Dalai Lama
